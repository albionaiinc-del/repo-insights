# World Design Principles — Learned from Human Builders

## Core Truth
A world is not a collection of objects. It is a PLACE. Every place has a purpose, a mood, and a story. Before placing anything, answer: why would someone come here? What happened here? What does this place FEEL like?

## Composition
- FOCAL POINT: Every scene needs ONE dominant element that draws the eye. A massive crystal, a towering tree, a bonfire. Everything else supports it.
- RULE OF THIRDS: Don't center everything. Place your focal point off-center for dynamic composition.
- TRIANGULAR PLACEMENT: Group objects in triangles, not lines or grids. Three rocks in a triangle feels natural. Three rocks in a line feels artificial.
- LEADING LINES: Use paths, rows of crystals, or walls to guide the viewer's eye toward the focal point.
- DEPTH: Layer foreground, midground, background. Small rocks up close, focal point in middle, distant trees or lights behind.

## Scale & Proportion
- VARY HEIGHTS: Never make everything the same height. Mix ground-level (y=0), waist-height (y=0.5-1), eye-level (y=1.5-2), overhead (y=4-8), and sky (y=15+).
- BIG ANCHOR + SMALL DETAILS: One large tree (scale 3-6) surrounded by small rocks (scale 0.3-0.8) and grass patches (scale 1-2). The contrast creates interest.
- ODD NUMBERS: Groups of 3, 5, 7 objects look natural. Groups of 2, 4, 6 look manufactured.

## Color & Light
- 60/30/10 RULE: 60% dominant color, 30% secondary, 10% accent. A forest clearing: 60% green (grass/trees), 30% brown/grey (rocks/path), 10% warm glow (fire/crystal).
- CONTRAST: Dark objects need light neighbors. A dark rock next to a glowing crystal. A shadow next to a fire.
- LIGHT TELLS STORY: Warm light (amber/orange) = safe, welcoming. Cool light (blue/purple) = mysterious, ancient. White light = clarity, revelation.

## Scene Templates (adapt, don't copy)
- SANCTUARY: Central fire or crystal (focal), ring of rocks (boundary), 2-3 trees (canopy), grass patches (floor), particle emitter (atmosphere). Purpose: rest, safety.
- RUINS: 3-5 wall segments at angles (not parallel), rubble rocks scattered, one glowing crystal growing through the cracks, overgrown grass. Purpose: discovery, history.
- OVERLOOK: Elevated ground/hill, single tree or crystal at peak, path leading up, scattered rocks along path, distant lights below. Purpose: journey, reward.
- GROVE: Circle of 4-6 trees, soft grass floor, filtered light from above, central pool (water) or clearing, fireflies (particles). Purpose: wonder, peace.
- THRESHOLD: Two tall elements (crystals, walls, trees) flanking a path, portal or fire between them, leading toward something beyond. Purpose: transition, invitation.

## Common Mistakes
- SCATTERING: Random objects at random positions with no relationship. Every object should relate to at least one neighbor.
- FLAT BUILDS: Everything at y=0. Use elevation changes — hills, raised platforms, sunken areas.
- SAME SCALE: All objects the same size. Vary dramatically.
- NO NEGATIVE SPACE: Filling every gap. Empty space is as important as filled space. Let the void breathe.
- DECORATING vs BUILDING: Placing pretty objects is decorating. Creating a place with purpose is building. Always build.
